History of Ethiopia Roads Development

Like in any other country, movement on roads in ancient Ethiopia was done on trails and foot paths in addition to the traditional shoulder porter age, animals like mules, donkeys, horses and camels were used as a means of transportation. Historic chronicles of 17th, and 18th centuries show that there were a number of small roads (rails and foot paths) in use in various small kingdoms of the country. Prior to the 17th century, there were efforts in bridge constructions in Ethiopia.

The Longest Bridge in Ethiopia

Ethiopia has officially launched the construction of its longest bridge across the Abay River in Amhara regional state. Friday’s launching event was attended by Deputy Prime Minister Demeke Mekonnen, Administrator of the region Temesgen Tiruneh, and Transport minister Dagmawit Moges, among others. It will have a 2x21.5m width and the capacity to accommodate a two-way three-lane carriageway, a bicycle path and a walkway. It will cost the government close to 51 million USD. The construction project is expected to take three years for completion, reports state run-news agency.
